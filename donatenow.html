Project Details

The 4 Channel Functional Electrical Stimulation (FES) System is a specialized therapeutic device designed to support patients undergoing neuro-physiotherapy, particularly those suffering from conditions such as stroke or hemiparesis. Stroke often leads to partial paralysis or significant weakness in one side of the body, affecting the ability to perform everyday tasks.

This advanced system uses electrical impulses to stimulate paralyzed or weakened muscles, helping retrain the brain and muscles for improved motor function. By delivering controlled electrical currents to targeted muscles in the upper limbs (UL) and lower limbs (LL), the device facilitates repetitive, goal-oriented movement patterns essential for recovery.

The implementation of this technology in neuro-physiotherapy enhances neural plasticity ‚Äî the brain‚Äôs ability to reorganize and form new neural connections ‚Äî thereby accelerating the rehabilitation process. It not only improves voluntary muscle control and strength but also enhances the quality of life for patients by increasing their independence in performing daily activities.

This project aligns with AMC's commitment to supporting inclusive health initiatives and advanced rehabilitation technologies under its CSR efforts, promoting equitable healthcare access for patients with neurological disabilities.
